What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website or use a web application. They allow the site to remember information about your visit. Not all cookies are the same — some are essential for the site to function, while others are used for tracking or advertising.

What We Do Not Use

This is a deliberate design decision. Your usage patterns within Portico are health-adjacent data. We don't think that data should be monetized or shared with advertising networks.

Analytics cookies (Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or equivalent)

Advertising or retargeting cookies of any kind

Social media tracking cookies

Third-party behavioral tracking

Fingerprinting or canvas-based device identification

Any cookie that shares your data with external parties

04

Managing Cookies

Because the cookies we use are strictly necessary for the Services to function, you cannot opt out of them while using Portico. If you disable all cookies in your browser, you will not be able to log in or use the platform.

You can clear your cookies at any time through your browser settings. This will log you out of Portico and reset any interface preferences you've saved.

05

Updates to This Policy

We will update this Cookie Policy if we ever introduce new cookies. We will notify you by email before any new cookies are set. The three cookies listed above will remain the complete set for as long as possible — minimalism is a design principle here, not an oversight.
